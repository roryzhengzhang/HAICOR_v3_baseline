Introduction

The primary goal of this study is to evaluate the usability of the baseline human-AI collaborative reasoning system. You will edit a commonsense reasoning path provided by the machine to become more rational, in your opinion. The system provides you with a basic interface to edit the reasonings.

Background

Tom was driving his car.

He wanted to take a scenic way home.

He deliberately passed his exit.

Tom saw many beautiful trees.

Tom took the scenic way home.

Given the story above, to answer the question "what is the human need that drives Tom to take the scenic way home?" The underlying AI will first identify an appropriate human need category (e.g., status, approval, food, etc.), then justify its choice with reasoning.

For example, it may believe that "calm" is the reason for Tom's behavior and provide a reasoning that

home related to rest related to calm

Every reasoning follows the same "concept relation concept" format. A concept can be a word or a simple phrase, while the relations can be one of the following types (which will be available during your editing process):

  • related to: concept 1 is like concept 2
  • is a: concept 1 is a type of concept 2
  • used for: concept 1 is used for concept 2
  • capable of: concept 1 can concept 2
  • causes: Sometimes concept 1 causes concept 2
  • has a: concept 1 has concept 2
  • has subevent: Something you do when you concept 1 is concept 2
  • has first subevent: the first thing you do when you concept 1 is concept 2
  • has last subevent: the last thing you do when you concept 1 is concept 2
  • has prerequisite: In order for concept 1 to happen, concept 2 needs to happen
  • has propertyï¼š concept 1 is concept 2
  • motivated by goal: You would concept 1 because you want to concept 2
  • obstructed by: concept 1 can be prevented by concept 2
  • desires: concept 1 wants concept 2
  • synonym: concept 1 and concept 2 have similar meanings
  • antonym: concept 1 is the opposite of concept 2
  • entails: if concept 1 is happening, concept 2 is also happening
  • manner of: concept 1 is a specific way of doing concept 2

A valid reasoning must begin with a concept that appeared in the story and end with one of the following human need concepts (which will be available during your editing process):

  • status
  • approval
  • tranquility
  • competition
  • romance
  • food
  • independent
  • power
  • serenity
  • honor
  • belonging
  • contact
  • rest
  • family
  • idealism
  • curiosity
  • savings
  • order
  • health

Your Task

Your task is to improve the system generated, problematic reasoning by

  1. Deleting inappropriate, in your understanding, concepts
  2. Replacing inappropriate, in your understanding, concepts with an appropriate one
  3. Adding new concepts
